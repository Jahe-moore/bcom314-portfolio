Five Required Dimensions — BCOM 314 Professional Persona Arc

Core Values Exploration

Identity Aligns. Leadership Label Does Not.

SPI: driven, hard-working, growth-oriented, analytical, pragmatic. PRS respondents describe a "Silent Leader" who earns credibility through action — a label I attribute to perfectionism and control, not leadership intent. Core values are aligned externally; the underlying process is not visible.

Gap: Small-to-Moderate. Others experience the outputs of my identity — not the reflective, growth-oriented process behind them. Drive and execution are recognized. Creativity and initiative in discussion are not.

Behavioral Communication Research

Competence Is Visible. Confidence Is Not.

2.27 Warmth Quotient
−2.27 Confidence Quotient

Charisma Diagnostic: Warmth Zone. "Warmth without confidence can lead others to perceive you as weak, ineffective." SPI composite: 0.0 Proactivity / −9.0 Reactivity. Analytical/Driver profile confirmed by PRS.

Gap: Moderate. Competence is evident in deliverables — rarely in real-time discussion. Mixed signals: peers see the output, not the thinking behind it.

Team Culture Findings

Strong Output. Low Presence.

"Jake did the bulk of our information assembly." — Round 2. "You do a great job with your part but it seems like you don't care about being part of the team." — Round 3. Meeting attendance: 2.4 → 2.25 → 2.0 out of 4. Positive team environment: 74 → 74.25 → 45.6 out of 100.

Gap: Large. Work-first culture (structured, output-centered) does not translate visibly into team engagement — the metric by which teammates assess contribution and build trust.

Identity – Brand – Reputation Alignment

Independent Achiever → Reliable Team Contributor.

Radian brand analysis: "There is a significant discrepancy between this strong individual performance perception and his team collaboration skills. His identity needs to evolve from an independent achiever to a reliable, high-performing team contributor who leverages data to solve problems and fosters a positive collective environment." The desired brand — competent, analytical, dependable — is accurate. The legibility of that brand in team settings is not yet consistent.

Work With / Hire ↑ Say Positive ↑ Rely On → Building Trust → In Progress Benefit of the Doubt → Earned Through Output

Strategy: Make thinking visible in real time. Speak from bullet points only. One substantive verbal contribution per meeting. The reputation outcomes I need — being relied on, trusted, hired — follow from presence, not just deliverables.
